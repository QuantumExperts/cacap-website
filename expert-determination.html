Expert Determination

Expert determination is a private, contract-based method of dispute resolution in which the parties appoint a neutral expert with specialised knowledge in a particular field to decide a dispute, typically of a technical or valuation-related nature. Unlike arbitration or mediation, the expert’s role is to apply their professional expertise to assess the matter and render a determination, which is often final and binding.

This process is particularly suited to disputes involving complex technical issues, such as those arising in construction, engineering, quantity surveying, programming and delay analysis, defects assessment, valuation, and contract interpretation.

The Centre maintains a panel of experts in structural engineering, civil engineering, mechanical and electrical engineering, quantity surveying, project management, programming and delay analysis, defects assessment, and construction law.

Key Features

  • Access to expert determiners with specialised technical knowledge.
  • Independent and impartial appointment process with rigorous conflict checking.
  • Determinations typically binding and final.
  • Confidential proceedings.
  • Efficient appointment process.

Process

The process commences when parties submit a request including the technical or contractual issues requiring determination, the required area of expertise, and any agreed qualifications. The Centre identifies candidates, conducts conflict checks, and appoints the expert determiner, who then manages all procedural matters independently.
