Nomination Fees & Expenses

Schedule of fees and expenses for CACAP nomination and appointment services.

Fee Schedule

1. Centre Fees

CACAP charges fees for the nomination and appointment services provided. These fees cover the Centre's administrative costs in identifying, conducting conflict checks, and appointing qualified neutrals.

  • Registration Fee: A non-refundable administration fee charged upon submission of a nomination request.
  • Nomination Fee: A fee charged for the Centre's services in reviewing its panel, conducting conflict-of-interest checks, and nominating or appointing a suitable neutral.
Indicative Fee Summary

Indicative Centre fees are set out below. These figures are provided for guidance only and may vary depending on the nature and complexity of the nomination request.

  • Registration Fee: USD 500
  • Single Arbitrator / Mediator / Expert Determiner Nomination: USD 2,500
  • Three-Member Tribunal — Presiding Arbitrator Nomination: USD 3,500
  • Expert Witness Nomination: USD 2,000
  • Dispute Board Member Nomination (per member): USD 2,500

All fees are exclusive of applicable taxes. For a detailed fee proposal tailored to your specific requirements, please contact info@cacap.asia.

2. Neutral Fees

The fees charged by the appointed neutral are separately negotiated and agreed directly between the neutral and the parties. These fees are determined by the neutral's experience, the complexity of the matter, and the time required.

CACAP does not receive any portion of the neutral's fees. Arrangements regarding the neutral's fees, payment terms, and any retainers are entirely a matter for negotiation between the parties and the neutral.

3. Administrative Services

Where parties request additional administrative support beyond the nomination service (such as assistance with procedural matters or post-appointment issues), additional fees may be charged at CACAP's then-current rates for such services.

4. Travel, Per Diem, and Reimbursable Expenses

The neutral's reasonable travel expenses, accommodation, meals, and other reimbursable expenses incurred in conducting proceedings are the responsibility of the parties. These expenses are separately agreed between the parties and the neutral and are not included in the Centre fees.

5. Taxes

All fees charged by the Centre are exclusive of any applicable goods and services tax (GST), value-added tax (VAT), or other taxes. Where applicable, tax will be added to Centre fees at the rate required by law in the relevant jurisdiction.

6. Deposits and Payment Terms

CACAP may require deposits or advance payments of fees to be made prior to or during the nomination and appointment process. Specific payment terms will be advised to parties upon submission of a nomination request.

7. Service Agreement

Centre fees and payment terms are governed by CACAP's standard Service Agreement, which is provided to parties upon nomination request. The Service Agreement sets out the full terms and conditions of the Centre's services.

8. Amendments

CACAP reserves the right to amend its fee schedule and terms from time to time. Notice of fee amendments will be provided to parties in advance where practicable.
