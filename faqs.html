Common Questions

Which process provides a binding and enforceable decision?
Arbitration enables parties to obtain a private determination that is both binding and enforceable. View Arbitration Process →
How does mediation help parties resolve construction disputes?
Mediation provides a confidential and collaborative process where a neutral mediator facilitates discussions, helping parties reach a voluntary and mutually acceptable agreement while preserving relationships. View Mediation Process →
Which approach is used for disputes involving technical expertise?
Expert Determination allows a neutral expert to assess complex technical or valuation issues and provide a binding decision based on specialised knowledge. View Expert Determination Process →
Who offers impartial technical advice during formal proceedings?
Expert Witness services provide independent analysis, reports, and testimony to assist tribunals or courts in understanding complex issues. View Expert Witness Process →
What functions do Dispute Boards serve?
Dispute Boards provide proactive oversight, guidance, and timely recommendations or decisions to prevent escalation, resolve disputes efficiently, and help maintain project relationships. View Dispute Board Process →
What happens if parties cannot agree on the appointment of a neutral?
CACAP provides nomination services, appointing a suitably qualified and impartial professional in accordance with the needs of the case. View Nomination Process →
What is CACAP accreditation and how do I become accredited?
CACAP operates a professional accreditation programme for dispute resolution practitioners. Accreditation is awarded to individuals who have successfully completed a CACAP training course and demonstrated exemplary competence in assessed practical sessions. View Accreditation Scheme →
