Expert Witness

Expert witness appointments involve the provision of independent, specialised knowledge and opinion by a qualified professional to assist in the resolution of disputes, typically in arbitration, litigation, or other legal proceedings. An expert witness is engaged to analyse complex technical or professional issues, prepare written reports, and, when required, provide oral testimony.

Expert witnesses must remain objective and impartial, and their primary duty is to the tribunal or court, regardless of which party engages their services.

The Centre maintains a panel of expert witnesses with professional qualifications in construction and project management, engineering, quantity surveying and cost estimation, programming, delay and disruption analysis, defects assessment, and contract administration and compliance.

Engagement of the expert witness, including terms of appointment, scope of instructions, fees, and contractual arrangements, is conducted directly between the nominating party and the expert witness.

Key Features

  • Experts with professional qualifications in relevant technical fields.
  • Qualified and impartial analysis and testimony.
  • Reports compliant with rules and ADR process requirements.
  • Technical support for arbitration, litigation, and mediation.

Process

Parties submit a request specifying required expertise, qualifications, and the nature of proceedings. The Centre reviews its panel, checks for conflicts, and nominates suitable expert witnesses. Parties then engage the expert directly.

Why Choose CACAP for Expert Witness?

  • Specialist Technical Expertise – Professional qualifications in construction, engineering, quantity surveying, programming, delay analysis, and related disciplines.
  • Expert Witness Experience – Experience preparing reports and providing testimony.
  • Professional Independence – Committed to objectivity and compliance with duties to tribunals and courts.
  • Direct Engagement – Parties engage directly with nominated expert witnesses.
