Membership Categories

CACAP offers professional membership to qualified dispute resolution practitioners across the Asia Pacific region. Membership is available in four categories:

Full Member
Associate Member
Student Member
Institutional Member

Eligibility

Full Members must be qualified professionals with experience in construction dispute resolution, arbitration, mediation, expert determination, or related dispute resolution services.

Associate Members are professionals with relevant professional qualifications or demonstrated experience in construction or related industries who are developing expertise in dispute resolution.

Student Members are students enrolled in approved construction, legal, or engineering programmes.

Institutional Members are organisations involved in construction, engineering, or dispute resolution services.

Benefits of Membership

  • Eligibility for inclusion on CACAP's professional panels and consideration for nomination or appointment
  • Listing in CACAP's register of qualified dispute resolution professionals
  • Access to member communications, panel opportunities, and notification of nomination requests
  • Opportunity to participate in CACAP professional development and training programmes
  • Networking with peers across the Asia Pacific region
  • Eligibility for CACAP professional accreditation upon successful completion of CACAP training courses

Application Process

  • Review Requirements: Confirm you meet the eligibility requirements for your chosen membership category.
  • Complete Application: Provide all required information regarding your professional qualifications, experience, and areas of expertise.
  • Submit for Review: Your application will be reviewed by the CACAP membership committee.
  • Approval Notification: Upon acceptance, confirmation will be sent to your email address.
  • Member Access: Access member resources and update your professional profile.
