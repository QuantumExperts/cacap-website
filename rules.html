Appointment Procedures

Rules and Process

The Centre’s role involves the nomination and appointment of qualified neutrals. This includes receiving appointment requests, reviewing panel qualifications, conducting conflict checks, and facilitating the appointment process in accordance with the Centre’s rules.

Once a neutral is appointed, the dispute resolution proceedings are conducted independently by the appointed neutral or panel in accordance with the applicable rules agreed by the parties. The Centre does not administer proceedings, manage case files, coordinate communications, or provide logistical support.

Arbitration

Step 1: Request for Appointment

Submit your dispute details to CACAP, including required expertise and any potential conflicts of interest.

Step 2: Nomination and Appointment

The Centre reviews its panel, checks for conflicts, and proposes or appoints a qualified arbitrator/tribunal.

Step 3: Commencement of Proceedings

The appointed arbitrator/tribunal manages proceedings according to agreed rules.

Step 4: Independent Proceedings

The tribunal handles all case management, evidence submission, and hearings.

Step 5: Award

The tribunal issues a binding award, enforceable internationally under the New York Convention.

Step 6: Post-Award Matters

Parties may request the Centre’s assistance for replacing arbitrators or resolving appointment disputes.

Mediation

Step 1: Request for Appointment

Submit dispute details, required mediator expertise, and any potential conflicts to CACAP.

Step 2: Nomination and Appointment

The Centre reviews its panel, checks for conflicts, and proposes or appoints a mediator.

Step 3: Commencement of Mediation

The mediator takes charge of proceedings according to the parties’ agreed rules.

Step 4: Independent Mediation Process

The mediator facilitates discussions, meetings, and negotiations.

Step 5: Outcome

Parties execute a settlement if agreement is reached; otherwise mediation concludes without determination.

Expert Determination

Step 1: Request for Appointment

Submit dispute details, technical issues, required expertise, and any potential conflicts.

Step 2: Nomination and Appointment

The Centre reviews its panel, checks for conflicts, and proposes or appoints an expert determiner.

Step 3: Commencement

The expert takes charge of proceedings under the parties’ agreed rules.

Step 4: Independent Determination Process

The expert manages submissions, inspections, meetings, and preparation of the determination.

Step 5: Determination

The expert issues a binding determination directly to the parties.

Expert Witness

Step 1: Request for Nomination

Parties submit a request specifying required expertise, qualifications, and the nature of proceedings.

Step 2: Nomination

The Centre reviews its panel, checks for conflicts, and nominates suitable expert witnesses.

Step 3: Direct Engagement

Parties engage the expert directly. Terms, fees, and instructions are managed between the parties and the expert.

Step 4: Independent Expert Work

The expert carries out their work independently.

Dispute Board

Step 1: Request for Appointment

Submit project details, board type, number of members, required expertise, and any potential conflicts.

Step 2: Nomination & Appointment

The Centre reviews its panel, checks for conflicts, and proposes or appoints board members.

Step 3: Commencement of Operations

The board assumes responsibility and operates according to the contract and agreed rules.

Step 4: Independent Proceedings

The board manages site visits, meetings, dispute hearings, and issues recommendations or decisions independently.
