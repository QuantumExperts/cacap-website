Arbitration Process

Step 1: Submit Request

Submit your dispute details to CACAP, including the nature of the dispute, required expertise, and any potential conflicts of interest.

Step 2: Panel Review and Conflict Check

CACAP reviews its panel of accredited arbitrators to identify qualified candidates, conducting thorough conflict-of-interest checks.

Step 3: Nomination or Appointment

CACAP nominates or appoints a qualified arbitrator or arbitral tribunal, subject to party agreement where required.

Step 4: Commencement of Proceedings

The appointed arbitrator or tribunal assumes responsibility for managing the arbitration in accordance with agreed rules.

Step 5: Independent Conduct of Proceedings

The tribunal manages all aspects of the proceedings including submissions, evidence, hearings, and deliberations independently.

Step 6: Award and Conclusion

The tribunal issues a binding and enforceable award. Parties may seek CACAP's assistance with replacement of arbitrators if required.

Mediation Process

Step 1: Submit Request

Submit dispute details and required mediator expertise to CACAP, including any potential conflicts of interest.

Step 2: Panel Review and Conflict Check

CACAP reviews its panel of accredited mediators to identify suitable candidates, conducting conflict-of-interest checks.

Step 3: Nomination or Appointment

CACAP nominates or appoints a qualified mediator, subject to party agreement where required.

Step 4: Commencement of Mediation

The appointed mediator assumes responsibility for facilitating negotiations in accordance with agreed rules.

Step 5: Independent Mediation Conduct

The mediator facilitates discussions, joint and separate meetings, and negotiations, assisting parties in reaching settlement.

Step 6: Conclusion

Mediation concludes with settlement agreement or without determination if parties cannot agree. The process remains confidential.

Expert Determination Process

Step 1: Submit Request

Submit dispute details, technical issues in question, required expertise, and any potential conflicts to CACAP.

Step 2: Panel Review and Conflict Check

CACAP reviews its panel of accredited expert determiners to identify qualified specialists, conducting conflict checks.

Step 3: Nomination or Appointment

CACAP nominates or appoints a qualified expert determiner, subject to party agreement where required.

Step 4: Commencement of Determination

The appointed expert assumes responsibility for the determination in accordance with agreed rules and procedures.

Step 5: Independent Expert Work

The expert manages submissions, technical inspections, expert meetings, and evaluation of evidence independently.

Step 6: Determination and Conclusion

The expert issues a binding determination directly to the parties, concluding the expert determination process.

Expert Witness Nomination

Step 1: Submit Request

Parties submit a request to CACAP specifying required expertise, qualifications, and the nature of the proceedings.

Step 2: Panel Review and Conflict Check

CACAP reviews its panel to identify suitable expert witnesses and conducts conflict-of-interest checks.

Step 3: Nomination of Candidates

CACAP nominates suitable expert witnesses for the parties' consideration and direct engagement.

Step 4: Direct Engagement

Parties engage the nominated expert directly. Terms, fees, and instructions are negotiated and managed between the parties and the expert.

Step 5: Independent Expert Work

The expert carries out their work independently, providing expert opinion evidence as instructed by the engaging party.

Step 6: Expert Opinion Provision

The expert provides opinion evidence for use in formal proceedings, subject to applicable court or tribunal rules.

Dispute Board Appointment

Step 1: Submit Request

Submit project details, board type, number of members required, expertise needed, and any potential conflicts to CACAP.

Step 2: Panel Review and Conflict Check

CACAP reviews its panel of accredited dispute board practitioners and conducts thorough conflict-of-interest checks.

Step 3: Nomination or Appointment

CACAP nominates or appoints qualified dispute board members, subject to party agreement where required.

Step 4: Commencement of Board Operations

The appointed board members assume responsibility for the dispute board in accordance with the project contract and agreed rules.

Step 5: Independent Board Operations

The dispute board manages site visits, project meetings, dispute hearings, and conducts proceedings independently.

Step 6: Recommendations or Decisions

The board issues recommendations or binding decisions as required by the contract, proactively managing disputes.

Nomination Service Process

Step 1: Submit Nomination Request

Parties submit request details to CACAP including the type of neutral needed, dispute nature, expertise required, and conflicts.

Step 2: Panel Review and Conflict Check

CACAP reviews its panel of accredited professionals and conducts comprehensive conflict-of-interest checks.

Step 3: Nomination and Appointment

CACAP nominates or appoints a qualified neutral, subject to party agreement where contractually required.

Step 4: Commencement of Proceedings

The appointed neutral assumes responsibility and manages proceedings in accordance with the parties' agreed rules.

Step 5: Independent Conduct

The neutral conducts proceedings independently. Parties may request CACAP's assistance with challenges or replacements if necessary.

Step 6: Conclusion

Proceedings conclude with the neutral's decision, recommendation, or determination as applicable to the dispute resolution method.
